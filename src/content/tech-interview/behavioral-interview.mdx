# Behavioral Interview

[How to Succeed in A Behavioral Interview](https://blog.pramp.com/how-to-succeed-in-a-behavioral-interview-796af6233dd1) A behavioral interview is used to evaluate how you react to real work situations.
It helps interviewers understand:
* your strengths and weaknesses
* how you solve problems
* how you deal with conflict
* how you work with others
These interviews are usually conducted by:
* recruiters
* managers
* senior team members

## Before the Interview
### Research the Company and the Interviewer
Before the interview, research the company.
Things to look at:
* the company’s products
* mission and vision
* the technology they use
* the product or team you might work on
You should also research **the interviewer**.
Check:
* LinkedIn
* GitHub
* projects
* interests or hobbies
This can help you:
* connect with the interviewer
* stand out from other candidates
People often remember personal connections more than technical facts.

### Prepare Your “Why”
Interviewers often ask why you chose a career in tech.
Prepare a **30-second elevator pitch** that explains:
* who you are
* what you do
* where you want to go
Example structure:
* Your background
* Your transition or interest in tech
* Your future goals

### Common Questions to Prepare For
Prepare examples for questions like:
* What is your greatest strength?
* What is your greatest weakness?
* Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a coworker.
* What makes you unique?
* Why should we hire you?
Write down stories or experiences that answer these questions.

## During the Interview
### Use the STAR Method
A good way to answer behavioral questions is the **STAR method**.
**S – Situation**
Describe the context or background.
**T – Task**
Explain your responsibility in the situation.
**A – Action**
Describe the steps you took to solve the problem.
**R – Result**
Explain the outcome and what you learned.

### Ask Questions
Remember that **you are also interviewing the company**.
Ask questions about:
* company culture
* work-life balance
* team structure
* development process
If something is unclear, ask for clarification.

## After the Interview
### Follow Up
Stay in contact with the recruiter.
If possible, send a **thank-you email** to the interviewer.
Example:
Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today about the position. I really enjoyed our conversation and learning more about the team.
This shows professionalism and interest.

### Stay Calm
Remember:
* breathe
* stay calm
* don’t interrupt the interviewer
* treat the interview as a learning experience
Each interview helps you improve for the next one.
